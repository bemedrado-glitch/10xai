Not vanity. The phrase is a useful organizing principle if we make it **measurable**. Here's the operationalization:

### 2.5 Sales operating principles (cross-cutting)

1. **Demo-gate everything ≥$1K total ACV.** Public floors disqualify the wrong fits, the demo earns trust for the rest.
2. **Every demo ends with one of three answers**: "yes / not now / never." No "let me think." Cadence is structured to force the decision.
3. **MEDDIC qualification mandatory** before the second meeting. If we don't have 4 of 6 letters by demo end, we requalify before a proposal.
4. **No proposals over email — every proposal walked through live.** Reading a proposal alone kills 60% of deals before they start.
5. **24-hr proposal SLA.** Everyone else takes a week. We win on speed.
6. **Founder-walks-the-floor:** Bernardo personally checks the first 3 weeks of every $5K+ engagement. After that, ops owns it.

## 4. Pricing Strategy — Van Westendorf Simulation Across 100 Personas

### 4.1 Why Van Westendorf

For a service business with no public market comparable (i.e., a 10XAI Engine bundle has no off-the-shelf SKU equivalent), traditional cost-plus pricing under-prices. Van Westendorf's Price Sensitivity Meter (PSM) anchors price on **buyer-perceived value**, which is the only signal that matters for a non-commodity SMB-services purchase.

PSM asks each respondent four questions per service:
1. **Too cheap** — *"At what price would you start to suspect the quality?"* (P_TooCheap)
2. **Bargain** — *"At what price is it a great deal?"* (P_Bargain)
3. **Getting expensive** — *"At what price does it start to feel expensive — but you'd still consider it?"* (P_Expensive)
4. **Too expensive** — *"At what price is it just out of the question?"* (P_TooExpensive)

Plot the four cumulative distributions over the price range. Two intersections matter:
- **Optimal Price Point (OPP)** — where the *Too Cheap* curve crosses the *Too Expensive* curve. Maximizes acceptance.
- **Indifference Price Point (IPP)** — where *Bargain* crosses *Expensive*. The "fair-perception" price.

The acceptable price range sits between **PMC** (Point of Marginal Cheapness, *Too Cheap* × *Bargain*) and **PME** (Point of Marginal Expensiveness, *Expensive* × *Too Expensive*).

### 4.2 Simulation methodology — 100 personas

Real PSM requires 200+ live respondents per service. We don't have that yet. What we *can* do is run a deterministic simulation grounded in segment-level economics, then validate against the live deals we close in Y1.

**Population (100 personas):**

| Segment | Count | Region split |
|---|---|---|
| Local Service · No Website | 18 | US 12 / BR 4 / LatAm 2 |
| Restaurant · Insta-only | 14 | BR 8 / LatAm 4 / US 2 |
| Auto / Taller | 8 | LatAm 6 / BR 2 |
| Clinic · Booking Bottleneck | 16 | US 10 / BR 4 / LatAm 2 |
| SMB · No CRM | 12 | US 7 / BR 3 / LatAm 2 |
| Pro Firm · Content Gap | 10 | US 6 / BR 3 / LatAm 1 |
| SMB · No Social | 8 | US 4 / BR 3 / LatAm 1 |
| SMB · Review Backlog | 6 | US 4 / BR 2 |
| D2C · Support Overload | 5 | US 5 |
| Pro Firm · RFP Throughput | 3 | US 3 |
| **Total** | **100** | **US 56 / BR 32 / LatAm 12** |

**Per-persona simulation inputs:**

For each persona we generate a willingness-to-pay (WTP) profile based on:
- **Segment base WTP** — anchor from segment economics (e.g., a dental practice with $200K/mo revenue has a different WTP than a 1-truck plumber)
- **Pain severity** (1–10) — rolled per persona; multiplies WTP
- **Region multiplier** — US 1.0×, BR 0.55×, LatAm 0.42× (PPP-adjusted)
- **AI fluency** (1–5) — high fluency = lower WTP for "done-for-you" (they'd DIY); low fluency = higher WTP

For each service, four price points generated as % of base WTP:
- P_TooCheap = 18% of WTP
- P_Bargain = 45% of WTP
- P_Expensive = 95% of WTP
- P_TooExpensive = 130% of WTP
(With Gaussian noise σ=12% of base, then sorted to enforce P_TC < P_B < P_E < P_TE)

Distributions are tabulated in **§4.4**. The methodology will be re-validated against live closed-won pricing data quarterly.

### 4.5 Pricing operational rules

1. **Floors are floors, not list price.** The demo conversation determines whether to anchor higher.
2. **Bundle discounting cap:** any 3 Engines bundled gets max 15% off list, signed for 12 months. Not negotiable below 15%.
3. **Annual prepay discount:** 10% off monthly retainer if 12 months prepaid.
4. **Localized pricing:** BR pays in BRL at PPP-adjusted ~55% of US. LatAm pays at ~42%. Public site shows USD by default; locale pages show local-currency equivalents.
5. **No "discovery discount."** New customers pay floor or above. The demo proves value; price doesn't.

## 7. Appendix — Van Westendorf Simulation Assumptions

For full reproducibility of the §4 results, the simulation assumes:

**Per-persona base WTP determination:**
```
base_WTP = segment_anchor × pain_severity × ai_fluency_inverse × region_mult
where:
  segment_anchor (per service tier, derived from segment economics — e.g. clinic Care Engine anchor = $2,400 setup based on $20K/mo avg booking-loss exposure)
  pain_severity ~ Beta(2,2) on [0.6, 1.5]
  ai_fluency_inverse ~ Triangular(0.85, 1.0, 1.25)
  region_mult: US 1.0, BR 0.55, LatAm 0.42
```

**Per-persona PSM price generation:**
```
P_TooCheap     = 0.18 × WTP × ε₁     where ε ~ N(1.0, 0.12)
P_Bargain      = 0.45 × WTP × ε₂
P_Expensive    = 0.95 × WTP × ε₃
P_TooExpensive = 1.30 × WTP × ε₄
sort_ascending() to enforce P_TC < P_B < P_E < P_TE
```

**Curve construction:**
```
Over price range [min(P_TooCheap), max(P_TooExpensive)]:
  pct_too_cheap(p)     = % of personas with P_TooCheap ≥ p          (decreasing)
  pct_bargain(p)       = % of personas with P_Bargain ≥ p           (decreasing)
  pct_expensive(p)     = % of personas with P_Expensive ≤ p         (increasing)
  pct_too_expensive(p) = % of personas with P_TooExpensive ≤ p      (increasing)

OPP = price where pct_too_cheap(p) == pct_too_expensive(p)
IPP = price where pct_bargain(p)   == pct_expensive(p)
PMC = price where pct_too_cheap(p) == pct_bargain(p)        [acceptable lower bound]
PME = price where pct_expensive(p) == pct_too_expensive(p)  [acceptable upper bound]
```

**Validation plan (quarterly):**
- Capture actual closed-won prices per tier per region
- Compute the 25th, 50th, 75th percentile of actual prices
- Compare against simulated OPP/IPP
- If actual median is <80% of simulated OPP for two consecutive quarters, the simulation is wrong; rerun with updated segment anchors

The simulation will be re-run end of each quarter with the previous quarter's closed deals as ground-truth for segment_anchor recalibration. The goal is convergence between simulated and observed pricing within four quarters.
