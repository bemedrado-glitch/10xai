## ★ Strategic decision for 10XAI: how to relate to GHL

Three plausible postures:

### Option A — **Build on GHL** (white-label, integrate)
- Use GHL sub-accounts to deliver client engagements
- Skip building CRM/funnels/automations from scratch
- Margin = $297 cost → resold at $597–997/mo to clients
- **Pros:** Speed-to-market in months, not years; mature platform; immediate revenue
- **Cons:** Vendor lock-in; commodity competition (50K+ agencies on GHL); no differentiation from infrastructure

### Option B — **Compete with GHL** (build proprietary platform)
- 10XAI builds its own thin platform with AI agents at the core
- Real moat over time
- **Pros:** True differentiation; full margin; localized for LatAm; defensible
- **Cons:** Years of dev; expensive; risk of feature gap

### Option C — **Hybrid: marketing + ops on custom, client delivery on GHL**
- 10XAI marketing site, free tools, lead engine = custom (Next.js + Supabase + Claude)
- Client engagements delivered on **GHL** for CRM/automation primitives + custom 10XAI agents on top
- **Pros:** Best of both — fast revenue + own brand
- **Cons:** Two stacks to manage

★ **Recommended:** **Option C (hybrid)**, with PT-BR / ES localization as the differentiation.

GHL doesn't speak Portuguese or Spanish. Its 1M businesses are overwhelmingly North American. **Brazil and LatAm are wide open at agency-platform tier.** 10XAI can become "GoHighLevel for LatAm SMB agencies" without rebuilding GHL — by wrapping it with localized AI agents, content, and a culturally-native delivery layer.
