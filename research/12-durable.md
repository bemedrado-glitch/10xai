## ★ Critical implications for the 10XAI flywheel idea
1. **Durable does NOT do outbound.** They don't find SMBs with no sites and pitch them. They wait for SMBs to come to them. **This is the entire whitespace.** 10XAI's pitch isn't "build a site" — it's "we already built your site, here it is, look at how it uses your reviews."
2. **Durable's customer is ALREADY a Durable customer.** Their 3M users are the *self-aware* SMBs who Googled "AI website builder." 10XAI targets the *unaware* SMB — the dental practice with 47 five-star reviews who hasn't thought about a website. Different funnel altogether.
3. **Durable's $25/mo is the price ceiling.** 10XAI's flywheel sells higher because it's *finished + sold* — likely $300–800 one-time + $29–59/mo hosting+updates. Durable validates the recurring exists.
4. **Don't try to build a Durable.** 10XAI doesn't compete with the platform; 10XAI **wraps** a platform (Durable, Webflow, Framer, Hocoos, or its own thin layer) for a *specific outbound motion*. This is also the cheaper path technically.
5. **Durable's vertical templates are reusable as data.** Each Durable industry template confirms a vertical with proven SMB demand. 10XAI's outbound list should prioritize the same verticals: home services, beauty, professional services, food/events, pet, auto.
6. **Localization is pure greenfield.** Durable + Hocoos have no PT-BR. Bernardo's trilingual edge becomes a structural advantage in months 0–18 before they enter LatAm.
