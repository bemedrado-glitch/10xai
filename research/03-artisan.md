## Weaknesses (gaps 10XAI can exploit)
- **No pricing transparency**
- **Vague accuracy/quality claims** — A/Z testing, intent signals, enrichment all generic
- **No vertical depth** — all "B2B" generic
- **English-only positioning** — no LatAm presence
- **Edge-case ROI claims** without baselines (the $100K number is from one quote)
- **Single product line** = single point of failure if buyer doesn't need outbound

## Implications for 10XAI
1. **Brand-led GTM is real.** Artisan's billboards proved provocative brand stunts work in this space. 10XAI's "Nike × Apple" energy must show up *outside* the site too — paid social, paid search, even local OOH in target US cities.
2. **The "AI as a hire" framing is sticky.** "Hire Ava" reads as a person. 10XAI should consider naming its agents (e.g., "Bia, the SDR" / "Carlos, the support agent") for memorability, especially in PT-BR/ES where personal-name agents are culturally even stickier.
3. **Press is leverage.** Artisan's press wins = trust shortcut. Phase 2 should plan PR/founder media in healthcare-AI press + LatAm tech press.
4. **Don't be all-things.** Artisan does ONE thing very loudly. 10XAI's six systems can dilute. Pick a flagship (likely the SDR or the "no-website flywheel") and make IT the marquee story.
