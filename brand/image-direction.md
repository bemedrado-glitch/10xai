# 10XAI Image Direction

> **Authored:** 2026-04-30 · For founder photoshoots, hero illustrations, brand visuals.
> Includes ready-to-paste prompts for AI image generators (Midjourney, Flux, ChatGPT, Adobe Firefly).

---

## Visual philosophy

10XAI's image system has three sources of truth:

1. **Bernardo Medrado** — the founder. His face and presence are the agency's #1 trust asset.
2. **Operations photography** — real workspaces, real signals (Google reviews, lit screens, notebooks, coffee).
3. **Editorial illustrations** — minimal, geometric, gold-on-black abstractions of the products (Lighthouse, Engines, Methodology, Multilingual reach).

What we **never** produce:
- Stock-photo "diverse team smiling at laptop"
- Robots, brains, neural networks, glowing chips
- Hands-on-keyboards top-down shots
- Cyan-blue futuristic UI mockups
- Purple/pink AI gradients

## Section 1 — Founder photography (Bernardo)

### Shoot 1: Studio (premium, controlled)

**Use case:** Hero band on Home, About page hero, LinkedIn/YouTube banners, press kit.

**Setup:**
- Backdrop: warm cream (matches `cream.DEFAULT` #FAFAF9) or charcoal (matches `ink.900` #1C1917)
- Lighting: soft key light + subtle warm fill; no hard shadows
- Wardrobe: dark blazer over crisp shirt, no tie; or dark crew-neck sweater
- Props: minimal — perhaps a single notebook, a watch, a coffee mug
- Aspect ratios needed: 1:1 (avatar), 4:5 (portrait), 16:9 (banner), 9:16 (mobile hero)

**Composition direction:**
- 30 final selects across:
  - 8 hero portraits (waist-up, eye contact, faint smile)
  - 8 thinking poses (looking off-camera, slight serious)
  - 6 cropped face/eyes shots for tight UI badges
  - 4 environmental-feel within studio (with notebook, gesture mid-thought)
  - 4 wide negative-space shots for hero overlays (subject left, copy space right)

**Mood references** (verbal — for the photographer):
- *Patagonia founder portrait* meets *The Verge tech-leader feature*
- Less: *Forbes 40 under 40 stock smile*
- Yes: *contemplative*, *operator*, *settled confidence*
- Skin tones warm; no cool/blue cast in post

### Shoot 2: Environmental (candid, on-brand)

**Use case:** About page secondary, blog header images, behind-the-scenes social content, methodology page.

**Locations** (one or all):
- Bernardo's home office or coworking space
- Walking through a downtown shopping district (the kind of street where SMB clients live — local restaurants, dental offices)
- At a real client site (after first case study lands)
- In transit (airport, café, on a call)

**Style:**
- Hand-held feel, slight motion blur OK
- Real light (window light preferred over studio strobes)
- Real moments (not posed)
- 30–40 selects, edited for authenticity not perfection
- Wardrobe: same range as studio but more relaxed (knit, button-down, jeans OK)

**Sample shots:**
- On phone, walking past a small business with a sign
- Taking notes in a café (overhead, hands + notebook)
- Looking at a Google Maps listing on a phone (extreme close-up)
- At a kitchen counter, laptop open, coffee
- Mid-conversation on a video call (over-shoulder)

## Section 2 — Brand hero illustrations

These are the **non-photographic visuals** for the homepage, sub-product pages, and content. Editorial-illustration style: flat, geometric, minimal palette.

### Style lock
- **Palette:** `ink.900` (#1C1917) + `gold` (#CA8A04) + `cream.DEFAULT` (#FAFAF9). Optional: `gold.100` (#FEF9C3) for subtle highlights.
- **Treatment:** flat vector or near-flat, no glossy gradients, no 3D
- **Composition:** lots of negative space, asymmetric, off-center subjects
- **Line:** medium-stroke (around 2–3px equivalent), consistent
- **Geometry:** clean circles, rectangles, lines — minimal organic shapes
- **References:** Stripe blog illustrations, Linear app illustrations, Notion early-2020s minimalism

### Required illustrations (4 hero pieces)

#### 1. Lighthouse hero
**Concept:** A literal lighthouse, abstracted. The beam is the gold accent, sweeping across the scene illuminating small business markers — pins on a map, building silhouettes, review stars.

**Midjourney / Flux prompt (ready to paste):**
```
Editorial flat-illustration of an abstract lighthouse, beam of warm gold light sweeping across a horizon of stylized small-business buildings — a dental clinic, a café, a bakery, an auto shop. Each business has a small five-star rating glyph above it. Negative space dominates the right two-thirds of the composition. Flat vector style, no gradients, palette restricted to deep warm black #1C1917, antique gold #CA8A04, off-white #FAFAF9. Wide aspect 16:9. Minimal, editorial, Stripe-blog-meets-Linear-app aesthetic. No text. No people.
```

#### 2. Engines hero
**Concept:** A 2×3 grid of geometric "engine" icons (one per service tier), connected by gold lines. Reads as a system architecture diagram, but elegant.

**Prompt:**
```
Minimal editorial diagram: six abstract geometric icons arranged in a 2x3 grid, each represented by a different simple shape — circle, square, triangle, hexagon, plus-sign, diamond. Thin gold connecting lines route between them. Background is a soft cream #FAFAF9. Icons rendered in deep warm black #1C1917 with one accent of antique gold #CA8A04 per icon. Flat vector style, no shadows, no gradients. 16:9 aspect. Editorial illustration, technical-but-warm. No text. No labels.
```

#### 3. Methodology timeline
**Concept:** Four vertical bars rising from short to tall (or four steps unfolding left-to-right), representing weeks 1–4 of the production protocol. Bars are gold, ground line is ink.

**Prompt:**
```
Minimal infographic illustration: four vertical bars in a row, each progressively taller, sitting on a thin horizontal line. Bars are antique gold #CA8A04, line and background dark warm black #1C1917. To the right of the tallest bar, a small geometric arrow pointing forward. Composition is editorial and clean, plenty of negative space, no labels. Flat vector style. 16:9 aspect ratio. Inspired by Linear/Vercel/Stripe blog graphics.
```

#### 4. Multilingual reach
**Concept:** Three speech bubbles, one with "EN", one with "PT", one with "ES" inside (or no text — abstract waveforms in different rhythms). Connected by a single line as one continuous conversation.

**Prompt:**
```
Editorial flat-illustration of three abstract speech bubbles arranged in a horizontal row, each containing a different waveform pattern (representing English, Portuguese, Spanish). The three bubbles are connected by a thin continuous antique-gold line that flows through them, suggesting one operator handling three languages. Off-white #FAFAF9 background, deep warm black #1C1917 strokes, antique gold #CA8A04 connecting line. No people. No text. Flat vector minimalism. 16:9 aspect.
```

## Section 4 — Operations / lifestyle photography

For blog posts, case studies, and social content. **Real photography, not stock.**

Subjects (in priority order):
1. **Real client locations** (after consent) — clinics, agencies, restaurants
2. **Tools in use** — laptop showing Dez interface, phone showing a Lighthouse preview, calendar showing booked appointments
3. **Founder at work** — see Section 1
4. **Abstract close-ups** — espresso, notebooks, sticky notes, hands on keyboards (only when contextual)

### Brief for case-study photography
When 10XAI lands its first case study client, commission a half-day photoshoot at the client location. Capture:
- Owner portraits (3–5 shots) — to feature alongside testimonial
- Workspace details (5–10 shots)
- Interaction with 10XAI tools (e.g., owner looking at Dez chat, scrolling new website on phone)
- Hero shot for the case-study landing page

Budget: $500–1,000 per case study. Worth every dollar — the case study is the trust unlock.

## Section 5 — Forbidden image patterns

**Never use any of these on the 10XAI surface:**

- ❌ Robot hands typing on a keyboard
- ❌ Glowing brain or neural-net imagery
- ❌ Person holding a phone with floating UI elements above it
- ❌ Group of stock-diverse people pointing at a screen
- ❌ Cyan/electric-blue futuristic dashboard mockups
- ❌ Purple/pink "AI" gradients of any kind
- ❌ "Hands typing on glowing keyboard from above"
- ❌ Robot faces or humanoid AI characters
- ❌ Any image with built-in text in a language we don't speak
- ❌ Generic stock from Unsplash/Pexels labelled "team meeting"
- ❌ Holographic interfaces
- ❌ Wireframe globes

If a vendor or AI generator returns any of the above — discard, don't iterate.

## Section 6 — How to ship images on a lean budget

### Founder photography
- One half-day photoshoot with a local Raleigh-Durham photographer, $400–800 all-in
- Bernardo styles himself; one wardrobe change
- Editing handled by photographer; ~30 final selects delivered

### Hero illustrations
- Generate v1 with Midjourney / Flux using prompts above ($30 subscription, 20 minutes)
- Optionally have a freelance illustrator clean up + vectorize ($200–400 each, or $600–1,200 for all four)
- Lean path: ship AI-gen as raster PNG behind `next/image`, swap to vector when budget permits

### Engine card illustrations
- Lucide icons + a gold accent ring around each works as v0 (free, immediate)
- Custom illustrations are a v1.5 upgrade

### Total
- **Tight (lean budget):** ~$700–1,500 for a complete v1 image system
- **Comfortable:** ~$2,000–4,000 for v1 + custom illustrations

Within the master strategy's brand-asset budget envelope ($1,400–4,200).
